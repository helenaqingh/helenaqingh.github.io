My Garden

I was born and raised in a small town in the southern part of China, Meizhou in Guangdong Province. It is known as the capital of the Hakka people, whose name means “guest people.” Our ancestors originally came from central China but migrated south during times of war. Each family might move at a different point in history; my family settled in Guangdong to escape the rebellion known as “安史之乱.”

Land means a great deal to us. We are thankful for the southern soil that provides food and life, and we also remember the land we had to leave generations ago. Wherever I go to study, I always try to grow something, even a small potted plant gives me comfort.

My parents take care of our garden back home. We have pomelo trees, peach blossoms, camellias, chili peppers, corn, and many other plants. Here are some photos of our garden.
